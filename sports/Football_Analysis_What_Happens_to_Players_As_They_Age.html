Topic of Analysis¶

How do player ratings and their attributes change as they age?

Summary of Findings¶

An evaluation of nearly ~10 years of player data, containing over 183K rows of information allowed me to come to the following conclusions:

1) After 30, things start to go downhill. Player ratings start a slow decline, but don't actually decline as quickly as I would have thought.
2) Player speed peaks around the age of 24 on average and then starts a rapid descent after age 30. There is a negative correlation between age and speed of -0.21.
3) Similar to speed, we see the mean decline as players age across their ability to finish, how well they see play on the pitch (vision) and shot power.
4) A player's potential seems to peak at the age of 24 on average.
5) Not all things decline as we age, the one thing which grows as players age is weight!
6) Also, not surprising to see that as players weight grows, their speed declines with a negative correlation of -0.38.

Conclusion and Key Learnings¶

So what happens as players age?¶

From the time that a player is approximately 16 years old, we begin to see a steady rise in their mean overall ability (rating). Players on average tend to peak in ability as they approach 30 and then gradually start to decline in ability.

Player potential along with Player Speed tends to peak early around the age of 25. Other abilities such as shot power, player vision and a players ability to finish tends to peak a bit later in life near the end of their 20's. Of the attributes measured, player speed and their ability to finish drops off the quickest vs. the peak. The correlation between speed and age was -0.21 overall which implies a weak linear relationship. This weak relationship is probably due to the rise and fall of speed as we age.

Of the 5 attributes analyzed, only one continued to grow as players got older, that being player weight. Player weight also had a mild negative relationship with player speed which isn't too surprising. As we get heavier, we tend to get slower.

Key Learnings about this Project¶

I learned a lot about how to use python for this project. Originally, the outline of the project recommended doing a lot of the querying within a sqlite program called DB Browser. I really enjoy using Jupyter for analysis and decided to search of a python sqlite library and found sqlite3. This worked out really well and I was able to quickly import each table into their own dataframe and start the analysis.

On the analysis side, I knew that as we age we decline in physical ability. This makes logical sense. What I didn't know was how different aspects of our physical abilities decline and at what time. This was what I was interested in and wanted to see the data behind these declines.

One other thing that kind of surprised me was how using the mean was much more visually effective at showing how player attributes and ratings change as they age vs. using a scatterplot. In hindsite, it makes sense, you get see the rise and fall of player ratings. But at the time I was originally inclined to do a scatterplot analysis.

Limitations & Other Analysis Considerations.¶

One of the key limitations of my analysis, was really unpacking why we see significant drop-off in player attributes but not seeing a similar drop-off in the mean players overall rating. I would love to understand what could be driving the slow decline in player ratings after 30. Better understanding how the overall rating was calculated would help me feel more comfortable with that finding.

I had originally included a multiple regression of the overall rating vs. all of the other attributes and found the 'reaction' attribute had the highest coefficient value. I decided not to include this in the analysis, but thought it would help me better understand what influences that overall rating. I was also unsure if my interpretation of that result was correct and would like to explore this again.
